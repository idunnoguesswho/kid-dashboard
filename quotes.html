B 🐙 Random Kid-Friendly Fun Fact

Octopuses have three hearts — and two of them stop beating when they swim! An octopus has one main heart that pumps blood to its body, and two extra hearts just for its gills. But those two gill hearts actually stop working when the octopus swims, which is why octopuses prefer crawling along the ocean floor. It's like if your heart took a little nap every time you ran — no wonder they'd rather walk.

C 📖 Merriam-Webster Word of the Day

Verisimilitude (noun) | veh-rih-sih-MIL-ih-tood

Definition: The quality of appearing to be true or real; the sense of believability in a story, statement, or depiction.

Example: The novel's verisimilitude was so convincing that readers kept writing to the author asking if the events had really happened.

Etymology: From Latin verisimilis — "resembling truth" — built from verus ("true") and similis ("similar"). Writers and philosophers of the Renaissance were especially fond of it when debating whether fiction had an obligation to mirror reality.

D 📅 This Day in History — April 1

1578 — William Harvey is born. The English physician who would later discover how blood circulates through the body was born on this day. Before Harvey's work (published in 1628), people still believed the ancient Greek theory that blood was consumed by the body like fuel.

1700 — April Fools' Day becomes widespread in Europe. By April 1, 1700, the tradition of pranks and "fool's errands" was firmly established across England and Scotland.

1826 — Samuel Morey receives a patent for a gas- and vapor-powered engine — a precursor to the engines that would eventually power automobiles.

1918 — The Royal Air Force is founded. Britain merged its Royal Flying Corps and Royal Naval Air Service into the world's first independent air force.

1976 — Apple Computer Company is founded. Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne signed the founding documents.

1999 — Nunavut becomes Canada's newest territory, representing one of the most significant acts of Indigenous self-determination in Canadian history.

H ⚖️ Marcus Aurelius / Stoic Reflection

"You have power over your mind — not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength." — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations

Marcus Aurelius wrote the Meditations as personal notes to himself — a Roman emperor reminding himself not to be swept away by flattery, outrage, or fear. The only domain where we are truly sovereign is our own inner life.

Practice for today: When something irritates or unsettles you, pause before reacting and ask: "Is this within my control?" If not, breathe and let it pass.

J 🌍 Britannica Fact of the Day

The Eiffel Tower grows taller in summer. Due to thermal expansion, the iron structure can grow by up to 15 centimeters (about 6 inches) on hot days. Nearly 7,300 tons of iron expand when heated and contract when cooled — making the Eiffel Tower measurably a different object in July than it is in January.
